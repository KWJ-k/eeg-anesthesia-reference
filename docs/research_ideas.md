# Research Ideas

## Research Topic 1

### Working Title

Age-related paradox of processed EEG under sevoflurane anesthesia.

### Observation

Near comparable age-adjusted MAC levels, increasing age was associated with:

- lower absolute EEG power across delta, theta, alpha, and beta bands
- higher BIS
- higher SEF

This pattern appeared in:

- age-bin averaged reference tables
- row-level 120-second EEG windows
- case-level averages
- MAC targets from approximately 0.8 to 1.2

### Working Interpretation

Older patients show reduced absolute EEG power, especially delta and alpha power.
However, relative redistribution of spectral power can increase SEF, and BIS may
not be age-neutral under volatile anesthesia.

### Candidate Claim

At comparable age-adjusted sevoflurane MAC, BIS and SEF increase with age despite
decreasing absolute EEG power.

## Research Topic 2

### Working Title

Limitations of spectral edge frequency as a standalone depth-of-anesthesia index.

### Observation

SEF is intuitive because it is frequency-domain based, but it can rise even when
absolute alpha and delta power fall. It is sensitive to power distribution,
patient age, agent type, device processing, and artifacts.

### Candidate Claim

SEF should be interpreted as an adjunct to raw EEG, DSA, BIS, SQI, and clinical
context, not as a standalone depth-of-anesthesia index.

## Next Validation Steps

- Repeat the age-BIS-SEF analysis for desflurane.
- Add sex-adjusted and sex-stratified analyses.
- Build mixed-effect models with case as a random effect.
- Add N2O and remifentanil/propofol covariates where available.
- Store DSA templates to visually validate frequency-domain summaries.
