# Literature Notes

## Age, MAC, BIS, and SEF

### Kanazawa et al., 2017

Title: *Electroencephalographic effect of age-adjusted 1 MAC desflurane and sevoflurane in young, middle-aged and elderly patients*

Key point:

- At age-adjusted 1 MAC, BIS and SEF95 in young patients were lower than in
  middle-aged and elderly patients.

Relevance:

- Supports the VitalDB observation that BIS/SEF can increase with age even near
  comparable MAC levels.

### Ni et al., 2019, British Journal of Anaesthesia

Title: *Paradox of age: older patients receive higher age-adjusted MAC fractions yet display higher BIS values*

Key point:

- Older patients showed higher BIS values despite receiving higher age-adjusted
  MAC fractions.

Relevance:

- Directly supports the age-related processed EEG paradox.

### Tsukamoto et al., 2020

Title: *Age-related effects of three inhalational anesthetics at one MAC on electroencephalographic waveform*

Key point:

- EEG amplitude decreases with age.
- SEF95 can be higher in older patients depending on the agent.

Relevance:

- Supports the idea that SEF can rise while absolute EEG power falls.

## SEF Limitations

### Jildenstal et al., 2022

Title: *Spectral edge frequency during general anaesthesia: A narrative literature review*

Key point:

- Reliable SEF intervals for adequate anesthesia are not well established.
- SEF depends on anesthetic agent, age, and clinical context.

Relevance:

- Supports using SEF as an adjunct rather than a standalone depth index.

### Tonner and Bein, 2006

Title: *Classic electroencephalographic parameters: median frequency, spectral edge frequency etc*

Key point:

- Classic single-number EEG parameters have interpretive limits.
- No single EEG index fully describes anesthetic depth across contexts.

Relevance:

- Supports the research topic on limitations of SEF.
